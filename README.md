Software engineer in Denver, 7+ years in. I started in Java and Spring Boot at Dish and Travelport, where I build the validation and data-integrity systems that keep millions of hotel records trustworthy — which mostly means catching the bad signal before it ever reaches anyone downstream. Somewhere along the way I fell hard for AI, and never looked back.

These days you'll find me up too late pair-programming with Claude or watching Ollama serve local models at 2am. I love this stuff. I also build **safeguards** for it — because I've watched AI get turned against someone I care about, and it turns out "I love AI" and "I want AI to be safe" are the same sentence. If there's a new model or framework out, I've probably already spun it up to see what it can do — and to see how it breaks.

### 🚀 What I'm Building

**⭐ Featured — [VerifyMyAI](https://github.com/end1989/verifymyai)** · [verifymyai.org](https://verifymyai.org) — *the one I'm proudest of*

A free, open-source tool that helps people detect hidden, coercive instructions — prompt-injection and jailbreak-style tampering — buried in their AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot). I built it after a bad actor quietly reconfigured a friend's assistant to gaslight him. It ships a detection-prompt library, red-flag patterns for **six manipulation tactics**, and trauma-informed safety (instant quick-exit, history scrub, always-on crisis resources) — and it runs **100% client-side with zero tracking**, so the people who need it most never have to trust me with their data.

`React 19` · `Vite` · `prompt-injection detection` · `trauma-informed UX` · `fully client-side`
